About

I was born in Provo, UT in the summer of 1998. I spent most of my childhood and adolescence in Spanish Fork, UT, where I graduated from high school in 2016. There I gained an interest in many hobbies, including snowboarding, guitar (click here to listen to my high school band), photography, weightlifting, and martial arts, which I still enjoy today. After graduation, I spent two years in Chile (el país más hermoso del mundo) performing religious service, where I learned to love the people and cultures of other countries and developed proficiency with the Spanish language. I returned from Chile in 2018, just weeks before beginning fall semester at Brigham Young University.

When I was young, my mother worked from home as a children's book illustrator (I'm sorry to say that I inherited little of her artistic talent). My father has spent most of his career working in the software industry (while also pursuing a variety of side projects). I gained my first real exposure to computer code at about ten years old, when my dad taught me how to write a webpage in html. The potential of new technologies and software, from self-driving vehicles to mobile applications, amazed me throughout high school, and I decided to pursue a career in Computer Science. I enrolled in the computer programming courses offered at my school and found an internship doing QA automation with C# after my junior year of high school.

I currently attend Brigham Young University, pursuing a bachelor's degree in computer science with a business minor and an emphasis in data science. I serve as a Vice President for the BYU Dev Club, and anticipate graduation in April 2022.

Family Map

Undergraduate Coursework

This project entailed an Android client in conjunction with a small custom server, each written in Java. The custom server communicates with a SQL database for persistent data. The primary activity of this application shows a map of all of the user's ancestors, indicated by pins on a Google Map activity, connected to each other by lines of different colors to indicate the relationship of that ancestor. The user can use filters to indicate which ancestors should appear on the map activity. The application included user registration, login, and session management.
